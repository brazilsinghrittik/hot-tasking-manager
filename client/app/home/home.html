How you can help

During diaster preparation, response and recovery, detailed geospatial information, maps of roads, buildings, bridges, etc is critical to dealivering the best service and support in the most timely and efficient way possible. Unfortunately this kind of information is not open and freely accessable for large parts of the world. Through coordinated, remote satellite or UAV imagery based mapping and a close working partnership with people on the ground you can help provide that data where and when needed.

New to mapping?

Anyone who can use a web browser to draw lines and boxes or use a phone to swipe and tap can meaningfully contribute to humanitarian mapping. For people using a computer (and a mouse helps a lot) who would like to help map by digitizing imagery, tracing buildings and roads, we ask you to visit our lean page which has one detailed video to get you up and mapping in about 30 mins.

Ready to get mapping?

If you are returning and ready to start mapping, please visit the Contribut page. You will see a list of mapping projects that need someone just like you to help get them done. You can select the Filter tab and narrow down the projects that you most want to work on. Your profile page has a list and quick link to projects you have already taken part in.

Field stories

A few of the humanitarian efforts you can support through mapping.

  • Southern Africa - Malaria

    "Malaria is a disease that affects millions of people around the world, particularly children under 5 and women. Malaria is prevalent in some of the most unmapped places. Malaria is a preventable and curable disease, but currently organisations do not have detailed data on the number of vulnerable people in an area, which reduces their ability to respond."

    Russ Deffner, HOT

  • Mozambique - Cholera

    "A cholera epidemic affects the Mozambique since the beginning of the year. Several regions are concerned and even the capital Maputo knows many cases. The peak of the epidemic appears behind us, but the country remains an endemic area for cholera. Some places in the north and in the west are regularly faced with killing waves. Therefore, it is highly necessary to proceed to a substantive treatment of the epidemic."

    Jan Bohm, MSF

  • Borno State Nigeria - Polio

    "The scope of the polio outbreak response for detected wild polio virus type 1 (WPV1) in Borno State has been expanded and different strategies employed to reach eligible children in Nigeria. The immunization campaign which commenced on 17 September, 2016 is targeting 31,540,054 children in 18 states across northern Nigeria. The area of Northern Borno is one vital area to this effort."

    Oluwaseun Egbinola
